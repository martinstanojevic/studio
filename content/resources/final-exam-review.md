## What happens in the classroom

The packet is intended as the last in-class session before the final exam. Students work individually for the first 50 minutes, then trade packets with a neighbor and self-grade against the answer key for the remainder. The teacher walks the room flagging which chapters each student is weakest on.

## Learning goals in detail

The exam itself mixes topics across chapters, so a chapter-by-chapter review has limited value at this point. The goal of this packet is precisely the *recognition* skill — given a word problem, can the student identify which technique applies? That skill is what most often breaks down on the real exam.

## Notes for instructors

Don't lecture during the working portion. The single best use of class time is the post-grading conversation: walk up to each student with their graded packet and identify the one chapter they should re-study tonight. A targeted suggestion is far more useful than another review lecture.

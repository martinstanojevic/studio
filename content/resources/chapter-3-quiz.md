## What happens in the classroom

The quiz is delivered asynchronously through the LMS — there is no in-class component. Students see immediate feedback on multiple-choice items and a rubric-based score on the two short-answer items after the window closes. Class discussion of the quiz happens the following day, focused on the items where the cohort underperformed.

## Learning goals in detail

The quiz checks both procedural fluency (computing summary statistics) and conceptual understanding (choosing the right measure for a skewed distribution). Two items deliberately give a skewed dataset and ask students to defend either mean or median — both can be correct with the right reasoning.

## Notes for instructors

Items 7 and 9 historically have the lowest correct-rate. If the cohort scores below 60% on those items, plan to spend 10 minutes of the next class re-teaching the median-vs-mean tradeoff. The dataset is generated fresh each semester from the synthetic survey generator in the course tools.
